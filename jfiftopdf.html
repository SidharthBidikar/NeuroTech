What is a JFIF File?

JFIF, or JPEG File Interchange Format, is a file format standard used to store and exchange JPEG-encoded images. It’s essentially a subset of the JPEG format, designed to ensure compatibility across different platforms and applications. JFIF files typically have the `.jfif` extension, though they are often interchangeable with `.jpg` or `.jpeg` files in most software. JFIF files are widely used for photographs, digital artwork, and scanned documents because they offer a good balance of image quality and file size through lossy compression.

While JFIF files are excellent for storing images, they can be cumbersome to manage when dealing with multiple files, especially for sharing or archiving purposes. Converting JFIF to PDF solves these issues by consolidating images into a single, universally compatible document format.
