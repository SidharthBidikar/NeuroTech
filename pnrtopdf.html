1. What is a PNR Number in Indian Railways?

A PNR, or Passenger Name Record, is a unique 10-digit number assigned to every ticket booked on Indian Railways. It's more than just a random string of numbers; it's a digital fingerprint for your entire journey. The PNR number stores crucial information about your travel, including:

  • Passenger Details: Name, age, sex, and berth preference.
  • Train Details: Train number, train name, origin station, destination station, and boarding station.
  • Journey Details: Date of journey, class of travel (e.g., 1A, 2A, 3A, SL), quota (e.g., General, Tatkal, Ladies).
  • Booking Status: Initial status at the time of booking (e.g., CNF for confirmed, WL for waiting list, RAC for Reservation Against Cancellation).
  • Current Status: The real-time status of your ticket, which can change as other passengers cancel or charts are prepared.
  • Coach and Seat/Berth Number: Assigned once the chart is prepared, usually a few hours before departure.
  • Fare Details: Total fare paid, and sometimes concession details if applicable.

The PNR number is generated by the Indian Railways' Computerized Reservation System (CRS) once a ticket is booked. It acts as a reference key for all interactions related to your booking, from checking status to lodging TDR (Ticket Deposit Receipt) for refunds. Understanding your PNR is the first step to a hassle-free train journey. Whether you need an "IRCTC PNR to PDF" or a general "Indian Railway PNR to PDF", this number is your key.
